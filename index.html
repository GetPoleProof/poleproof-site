The problem

You have no independent way to check the bill.

Pole owners write the make-ready estimate, perform the work, and issue the final invoice, with no outside party checking their math against the rules that are supposed to govern it. Overcharges, duplicate line items, and pole-replacement costs shifted onto the wrong party are common and hard to catch without reading every invoice against your agreement and the governing rules, line by line, on every pole, every time.

Methodology

What the audit actually checks

Every invoice runs through four independent checks. Each catches a different category of error, and they compound: a charge can pass one and still fail another.

01

Agreement match

Line items are compared against the rates, cost-share terms, and scope in your signed attachment agreement.

02

Subpart J compliance

Charges are checked against FCC Part 1 Subpart J cost-allocation rules, including which party owes what share.

03

Regional benchmark

Costs are compared against a growing dataset of what comparable work actually bills at, by pole owner and region.

04

Duplicate and drift

Invoices are checked against prior estimates and invoices for the same pole to catch duplicate billing and quiet cost creep.

Why PoleProof wins the argument

You only see your own invoices. We see the whole map.

One builder cannot tell whether a charge is fair. We can. Every line you send is compared against a growing benchmark of what pole owners across your region actually bill, so you know the moment you are charged above the norm, and you have the number to prove it. That is the difference between disputing a charge on a hunch and disputing it with evidence.

Every audit we run makes that benchmark sharper, which makes your next dispute stronger. It compounds for you, not for the pole owner.

Why PoleProof exists

You should keep the money that is yours.

I spent my career on the building side of fiber, and I watched the same thing happen on project after project. The pole owner writes the make-ready estimate, performs the work, and sends the invoice, and the builder has no independent way to know whether any of it is correct. The numbers ran high, sometimes far past what the rules allow, and there was never enough time to challenge every line.

That is a broken arrangement. The party you pay is the same party that decides what you owe, and it grades its own math. Every finding PoleProof returns is tied to a source you can point to: the specific clause in your agreement, the FCC Part 1 Subpart J cost-allocation rule, or the regional benchmark. Nothing is asserted without a citation.

PoleProof is the independent check I always wanted on that side of the table. You risk nothing to find out. Find nothing, owe nothing. Find something, and you keep the larger share.

Questions

Straight answers.

What does it cost? +
The first single-invoice check is free. A full recovery audit runs on contingency, a share of what you recover or avoid, with nothing upfront. If we find nothing, you pay nothing.
How do you check invoices without human reviewers? +
Every invoice is parsed and checked against your signed agreement, FCC Part 1 Subpart J cost-allocation rules, and a regional benchmark dataset that grows with every audit we run. The pipeline runs the same way whether it's your first invoice or your thousandth.
Do I need to talk to anyone to get started? +
No. You sign up online, upload your documents, and get your results by email. There's no call, no meeting, and no sales conversation at any tier, including Managed Monitoring.
What happens after you find an overcharge? +
You get a dispute-ready packet by email: the specific line items, the rule or benchmark each one violates, and the corrected amount. It's ready to send to the pole owner yourself.
Which monitoring plan is right for me? +
Pick by the size of your build. Starter covers a single build up to 1,000 poles with one pole owner. Regional covers up to 5,000 poles across as many as three pole owners, which fits most publicly funded builds, at an 18% share. Portfolio covers unlimited poles and pole owners at 15%. Move up as your build grows; your first month is credited against your first recovery.
Is my invoice and agreement data kept confidential? +
Yes. Your raw documents and anything identifying you are never shared with other clients or with pole owners. We use only de-identified, aggregated data points to improve the regional benchmark that strengthens every client's audit. Full detail is in our Privacy Policy.
