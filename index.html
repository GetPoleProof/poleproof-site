FAQ

Common questions, answered plainly.

What does PoleProof do?

PoleProof reviews make-ready and pole attachment invoices or estimates against your signed pole attachment agreement and the cost-allocation rules that actually apply — 47 U.S.C. §224 and 47 CFR Part 1, Subpart J for FCC-regulated poles, or the applicable state rule for cooperative and municipal poles — and returns a dispute-ready findings report.

How much does an audit cost right now?

Nothing. PoleProof is running a small number of free concierge audits to validate the model with real invoices before anything is automated or scaled. There's no subscription and no obligation.

What do I need to send to get an audit?

One invoice or cost estimate, plus your signed pole attachment agreement. Prior invoices help but aren't required.

Can you review an estimate before I pay it, or only invoices I've already received?

Both. Review an estimate before you pay, or audit an invoice you've already received — either works.

How long does an audit take?

A findings report typically comes back within 10 business days.

Is a findings report the same as a legal filing?

No. It's a factual and contractual review, not legal advice or a legal filing. If you decide to dispute a charge with the FCC or a state regulator, that filing should be prepared by your own counsel.

Who is PoleProof for?

Fiber ISPs, electric cooperatives, municipal broadband builders, and BEAD subgrantees working through make-ready and pole attachment costs.

Is my data kept confidential?

Yes. Documents you send are reviewed solely to produce your findings report and aren't shared beyond that purpose, other than in de-identified, aggregated form to improve PoleProof's benchmark data. See the Privacy Policy for details.
