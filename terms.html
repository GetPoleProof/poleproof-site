What PoleProof does

PoleProof reviews make-ready and pole attachment invoices or estimates you provide against your signed pole attachment agreement and the cost-allocation rules that apply to the relevant pole owner (47 U.S.C. §224 and 47 CFR Part 1, Subpart J for FCC-regulated poles, or applicable state rules for cooperative and municipal poles), and returns a findings report.

What a findings report is — and isn't

A PoleProof findings report is a factual and contractual review, not a legal filing, legal advice, or a guarantee of recovery. If you choose to dispute a charge or escalate to the FCC or a state regulator based on our findings, that filing should be prepared by your own counsel.

The current free concierge phase

Audits performed during the current validation phase are provided at no cost and with no obligation on your part. Submitting an invoice for audit does not create any payment obligation, and PoleProof does not require a signed contract to perform a free concierge audit — though we may ask you to sign our NDA before reviewing sensitive documents.

Confidentiality

Documents you send us are used solely to produce your findings and, in de-identified aggregate form, to improve our benchmark data. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Your responsibilities

  • The documents you send should be your own, or ones you're authorized to share.
  • You're responsible for any decision to dispute, pay, or escalate a charge — PoleProof's findings inform that decision but don't make it for you.

Limitation of liability

PoleProof provides findings based on the documents you supply and does not independently verify facts outside those documents (such as field conditions). We're not liable for decisions made based on a finding, or for outcomes of any dispute or regulatory filing you pursue.

Changes to these terms

We'll update this page as the model moves from free concierge audits to contingency-fee engagements, and will ask for a separate signed agreement before that shift applies to your account.
