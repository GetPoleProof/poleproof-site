Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

Draft, last edited July 2026. Not yet reviewed by counsel.
This is a working draft pending attorney review, published here so prospective clients, contractors, and developers can read the terms before sharing anything confidential. By submitting documents through the PoleProof intake form and checking the acknowledgment, you and PoleProof agree to these terms. It will be revised as that review is completed.

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into between PoleProof, a service of PoleProof LLC ("PoleProof" or the "Company"), and the individual or entity accepting it (the "Receiving Party"), each a "Party" and together the "Parties." It takes effect on the date you accept it, whether by checking the acknowledgment on the PoleProof intake form or by otherwise beginning to exchange confidential information.

1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship, such as a concierge audit of the Receiving Party's make-ready or pole attachment invoices, or related contract or development work, and in connection with that relationship each Party may disclose certain confidential information to the other.

2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party, whether in writing, orally, or by inspection, including but not limited to business plans, pricing, audit methodology, scoring or rule logic, benchmark data, client lists, invoices, agreements, financial information, and technical or product information. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction.

3. Obligations

The Receiving Party agrees to: (a) hold the Confidential Information in confidence; (b) not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent; (c) use it solely for the purpose described above; and (d) protect it with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care.

4. No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any rights to the other's intellectual property, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the stated purpose.

5. Term

This Agreement remains in effect for two (2) years from the effective date, unless earlier terminated by mutual written agreement. The confidentiality obligations survive termination for three (3) years following disclosure of the relevant Confidential Information.

6. Return or Destruction

Upon written request, the Receiving Party will return or destroy all materials containing Confidential Information.

7. No Obligation

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to proceed with any business relationship, transaction, or further disclosure.

8. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Maryland, without regard to its conflict of law principles.

9. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes any prior discussions on the subject.
